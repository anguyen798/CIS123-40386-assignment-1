Ranking of five card decks ranked by kickstarter funding including website link

  1. The Name of Wind Art Deck

    With $630,300 fundraised. Price: $15.00. Based on Patrick Rothfuss' acclaimed novel, the deck of cards also received his endorsement. Made by Echo Chernik, the deck showcased characters from The Name of the Wind, the initial book of the Kingkiller Chronicles series and is illustrated with a vibrant and colorful style.

  2. The Photography Deck

    With $364,000 fundraised. Price: $24.90. The Photograph deck combines the world of photography and playing cards. Made by Chris Ovdiyenko, each numbered card features essential photography techniques and rules, while the court cards boast individual camera designs, serving as both photography tip cards and playing cards. The deck is divided into four different categories, with each suit representing a specific aspect of photography. Spades showcase Camera Basics, Hearts represent Composition, Clubs embody Technical aspects, and Diamonds showcase Shooting Styles.

  3. Arcana

    With $190,592 fundraised. Price $34.99. The Arcana deck is a deck of cards inspired by Tarot (not 78 cards), utilizing its symbols and imagery. Made by Chris Ovdiyenko, it incorporates regular pips alongside Tarot elements like wands, swords, cups, and pentacles, and features four Major Arcana cards (Fool, Lovers, Death, Tower) instead of Jokers and ad cards.

  4. Federal 52

    With $188,005 fundraised. Price $45.00. The Federal 52 deck is a deck of cards inspired by currency and bank notes. Made by Jackson Robinson, it used traditional engraving designs from bank notes for the deck's style.

  5. Muertos Day of the Dead

    With $159,706 fundraised. Price $22.00. Muertos is a luxury set of American playing cards inspired by the Dia De Los Muertos holiday. Made by Steve Minty, the deck incorporates traditional aesthetics and the designer's personal experiences, portraying social classes and history of the culture while exuding contemporary elegance.
